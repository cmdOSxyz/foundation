# Human–AI Collaboration

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical
Category: Foundation

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the collaboration model between humans and Artificial Intelligence within cmdOS.

It establishes the division of responsibilities, authority boundaries, approval mechanisms, and governance principles that ensure AI remains a trusted execution partner rather than an autonomous decision-maker.

This collaboration model applies to every subsystem defined throughout the cmdOS Specification.

---

# 2. Core Principle

The relationship between humans and AI is collaborative.

Humans define purpose.

AI performs execution.

Neither replaces the other.

```
Human

↓

Intent

↓

cmdOS

↓

Execution

↓

Outcome

↓

Human
```

---

# 3. Human Responsibilities

Humans remain responsible for every decision involving intent, authority, ownership, and accountability.

These responsibilities include:

- defining objectives
- approving execution
- granting permissions
- setting policies
- establishing constraints
- evaluating outcomes
- accepting risk

These responsibilities cannot be delegated permanently.

---

# 4. AI Responsibilities

AI exists to reduce operational complexity.

Its responsibilities include:

- understanding intent
- planning execution
- selecting capabilities
- coordinating workflows
- monitoring progress
- recovering from failures
- optimizing future execution

AI executes.

It does not own objectives.

---

# 5. Principle of Human Authority

Within cmdOS, authority always originates from humans.

AI possesses delegated authority only.

Delegated authority:

- is explicit
- is revocable
- is limited
- is observable
- expires when appropriate

Authority is never assumed.

---

# 6. Levels of Autonomy

cmdOS defines five execution levels.

## Level 0 — Advisory

AI only provides recommendations.

Execution is entirely manual.

---

## Level 1 — Assisted Execution

AI prepares execution.

Human explicitly confirms every action.

---

## Level 2 — Delegated Execution

AI executes predefined actions within approved limits.

Unexpected situations require approval.

---

## Level 3 — Supervised Autonomy

AI independently performs routine operations while continuously reporting progress.

Critical decisions remain subject to human approval.

---

## Level 4 — Trusted Autonomy

AI manages complete workflows within established governance policies.

Human oversight remains continuously available.

No system may operate beyond this level.

---

# 7. Approval Model

Execution requiring elevated authority must receive explicit approval.

Typical approval events include:

- financial transactions
- identity verification
- permission escalation
- irreversible operations
- external communication
- legal commitments
- security-sensitive actions

Approval is a governance requirement rather than a usability feature.

---

# 8. Trust Relationship

Trust is established through transparency.

cmdOS must continuously expose:

- current objective
- execution plan
- selected capabilities
- execution status
- policy evaluations
- detected risks
- recovery actions

Users should understand what the system is doing and why.

# 10. Human Override

Humans may interrupt execution at any time.

Override capabilities include:

- pause
- resume
- cancel
- modify objective
- revoke permissions
- terminate workflows

No execution process may prevent human intervention.

---

# 11. Continuous Collaboration

Human-AI interaction is iterative rather than transactional.

```
Intent

↓

Planning

↓

Execution

↓

Observation

↓

Feedback

↓

Improved Execution
```

Every completed objective improves future collaboration without reducing human authority.

---

# 12. Collaboration Principles

Human–AI collaboration within cmdOS follows these principles.

### Human-Centered

Technology adapts to people.

People do not adapt to technology.

---

### Explainability

AI explains important decisions whenever appropriate.

---

### Transparency

Execution remains observable.

Hidden execution is prohibited.

---

### Predictability

Users should understand expected behavior before execution begins.

---

### Recoverability

Errors should be correctable whenever technically possible.

---

### Consent

Authority requires informed consent.

Consent may be withdrawn at any time.

---

# 13. Architectural Consequences

Because cmdOS is based on collaborative intelligence, the platform requires:

- Identity Framework
- Permission Framework
- Policy Engine
- Approval Engine
- Audit System
- Trust Framework
- Recovery Framework
- Human Override Interface

These components are mandatory.

---

# 14. Long-Term Vision

As AI capabilities improve, the collaboration model should evolve toward greater efficiency without reducing human governance.

The objective is not unrestricted autonomy.

The objective is trusted delegation.

Human authority remains constant.

AI capability increases.

---

# 15. Collaboration Invariants

The following statements shall always remain true.

1. Humans define objectives.
2. Humans retain final authority.
3. AI executes delegated work.
4. Authority is explicit.
5. Authority is revocable.
6. Every action is observable.
7. Approval precedes sensitive execution.
8. Human override is always available.
9. Accountability remains attributable.
10. Trust is earned through transparency.

No lower-level specification may violate these invariants.

---

# 16. Relationship to the Specification

This document establishes the governance relationship between humans and AI.

Subsequent specifications—including Identity, Permissions, Policy Engine, Approval Engine, Agent Runtime, Workflow Engine, Automation Engine, and Desktop Runtime—shall implement the principles defined herein.

---

# 17. Summary

Human–AI collaboration is the foundation of cmdOS.

Humans provide intent, authority, and accountability.

AI provides planning, execution, optimization, and recovery.

The objective is not autonomous replacement of humans.

The objective is trustworthy collaboration that transforms human intent into secure, observable, and reliable execution.
