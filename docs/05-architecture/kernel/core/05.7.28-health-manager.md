# cmdOS Context
# Part 10.35.28 — Health Manager

Version: 1.0
Status: Stable
Category: Kernel Observability

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the Health Manager of cmdOS.

The Health Manager is the canonical subsystem responsible for continuously evaluating the operational health of every kernel component, runtime, dependency, plugin, AI provider and distributed service.

It answers one question:

**"Is the system capable of operating correctly right now?"**

Health evaluation enables self-healing, autonomous operation and intelligent recovery.

---

# 2. Philosophy

Health is continuously evaluated.

Failures are expected.

Degradation is observable.

Recovery begins with accurate health information.

Health reflects reality, not assumptions.

---

# 3. Canonical Principle

Every health evaluation follows

Signal Collection

↓

Component Evaluation

↓

Dependency Evaluation

↓

Health Scoring

↓

Classification

↓

Publication

↓

Alerting

↓

Recovery Trigger

↓

Continuous Monitoring

Health evaluation never interferes with execution.

---

# 4. Responsibilities

The Health Manager is responsible for

- Component health monitoring
- Dependency health monitoring
- Runtime health evaluation
- Service health aggregation
- Predictive health scoring
- Health publication
- Alert generation
- Recovery triggering
- Health history
- Health auditing

The Health Manager does not

- Execute recovery
- Modify runtime state
- Route requests
- Evaluate permissions

# 19. Trust Boundary

Health information reflects observed operational state.

Health evaluations never modify runtime behavior.

Recovery systems consume health information but remain independently responsible for corrective actions.

---

# 20. Architectural Invariants

1. Every monitored component has a health state.
2. Health evaluation is continuous.
3. Health states are deterministic.
4. Dependency health contributes to aggregate health.
5. Every health transition generates an immutable event.
6. Health monitoring never blocks execution.
7. Health scores are reproducible.
8. Predictive analysis never overrides observed state.
9. Health history remains auditable.
10. Health information is namespace-aware.

---

# 21. Summary

The Health Manager is the operational health authority of cmdOS.

By continuously evaluating components, dependencies, resources and distributed services, it enables real-time monitoring, predictive failure detection, autonomous recovery initiation and reliable self-healing while preserving deterministic behavior and comprehensive observability across the Kernel.
