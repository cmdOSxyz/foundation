# 1. Purpose

This document defines the Disaster Recovery subsystem of cmdOS.

The Disaster Recovery subsystem is the canonical architecture responsible for restoring platform operations following catastrophic failures while preserving integrity, availability and business continuity.

It answers one question:

**"How can cmdOS continue operating when critical infrastructure fails?"**

The Disaster Recovery subsystem coordinates recovery across infrastructure, services, data, agents and workflows to minimize downtime and prevent permanent data loss.

---

# 2. Philosophy

Failure is inevitable.

Recovery is planned.

Continuity is measurable.

Automation reduces downtime.

Integrity is never sacrificed.

Every recovery is verifiable.

---

# 3. Canonical Principle

Every disaster recovery process follows

Failure Detection

↓

Impact Assessment

↓

Recovery Planning

↓

Failover

↓

Service Restoration

↓

Data Validation

↓

Health Verification

↓

Traffic Recovery

↓

Normal Operations

Every recovery remains deterministic and auditable.
