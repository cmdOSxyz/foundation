# Reasoning

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical
Category: Foundation

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of **Reasoning** within cmdOS.

Reasoning is the canonical cognitive process through which cmdOS transforms information into justified decisions, plans, conclusions, or actions.

Reasoning combines Knowledge, Memory, Context, Policies, and one or more Inference operations to solve objectives while remaining governed, observable, and explainable.

Every intelligent decision within cmdOS shall originate from one or more Reasoning processes.

---

# 2. Definition

A **Reasoning** is a governed cognitive process that evaluates available information to derive conclusions supporting an objective.

Reasoning may utilize:

- Knowledge
- Memory
- Context
- Policies
- Constraints
- Goals
- Inference
- External Information

Reasoning produces understanding.

Execution produces action.

---

# 3. Reasoning Is Not Inference

Inference performs computation.

Reasoning performs cognitive decision-making.

```
Reasoning

↓

Inference

↓

Intermediate Result

↓

Inference

↓

Conclusion
```

Reasoning may require multiple Inference operations.

Inference alone does not constitute Reasoning.

---

# 4. Reasoning Is Not Planning

Planning organizes future work.

Reasoning determines what should happen.

```
Reasoning

↓

Decision

↓

Planning

↓

Execution
```

Planning depends upon Reasoning.

---

# 5. Reasoning Is Not Execution

Reasoning determines actions.

Execution performs actions.

```
Reasoning

↓

Decision

↓

Execution
```

Reasoning may complete without initiating Execution.

---

# 6. Reasoning Characteristics

Every Reasoning process shall be:

- goal-oriented
- governed
- explainable
- observable
- reproducible
- traceable
- deterministic when configured

Reasoning shall preserve decision transparency.

---

# 7. Reasoning Identity

Every Reasoning process shall possess a globally unique identifier.

Minimum metadata includes:

- Reasoning ID
- Objective
- Execution ID
- Session ID
- Status
- Strategy
- Start Timestamp
- Completion Timestamp

Identity enables governance and auditing.

---

# 8. Reasoning Lifecycle

Every Reasoning process progresses through a managed lifecycle.

```
Created

↓

Initialized

↓

Evaluating

↓

Concluding

↓

Completed

↓

Archived
```

Alternative terminal states include:

- Failed
- Cancelled
- Interrupted

Lifecycle transitions shall remain observable.

---

# 9. Reasoning Inputs

Reasoning may consume:

- Knowledge
- Memory
- Context
- Policies
- Objectives
- Constraints
- User Intent
- External Information

Inputs remain governed throughout the Reasoning process.

---

# 10. Reasoning Outputs

Reasoning may produce:

- decisions
- plans
- recommendations
- explanations
- classifications
- priorities
- hypotheses
- confidence assessments

Outputs may become inputs for Planning or Execution.

---

# 11. Reasoning Strategies

Reasoning may employ multiple strategies.

Examples include:

- deductive reasoning
- inductive reasoning
- abductive reasoning
- probabilistic reasoning
- constraint-based reasoning
- rule-based reasoning
- comparative reasoning
- causal reasoning

Additional strategies may be introduced without changing the canonical model.

---

# 12. Reasoning Constraints

Reasoning shall respect applicable constraints.

Examples include:

- organizational policies
- security requirements
- legal requirements
- execution budgets
- resource limits
- capability restrictions

Constraints influence conclusions.

They do not replace Reasoning.

---

# 13. Reasoning Explainability

Reasoning shall support explanation.

Explanations may include:

- supporting evidence
- referenced Knowledge
- applied Policies
- evaluated constraints
- confidence indicators
- decision rationale

Explainability shall remain independent of implementation details.

---

# 14. Reasoning Observability

Every significant Reasoning event generates telemetry.

Examples include:

- initialized
- knowledge retrieved
- inference requested
- conclusion generated
- explanation produced
- completed
- failed

Reasoning history shall remain permanently observable.

---

# 15. Reasoning Governance

Reasoning remains governed throughout execution.

Governance includes:

- policy enforcement
- permission validation
- compliance
- auditing
- explainability requirements
- safety controls

Governance establishes trustworthy decision-making.

---

# 16. Reasoning Quality

Reasoning quality may be evaluated through measurable indicators.

Examples include:

- correctness
- consistency
- completeness
- explainability
- confidence
- reproducibility

Quality evaluation remains implementation independent.

---

# 17. Reasoning Composition

Complex Reasoning may consist of multiple Reasoning processes.

```
Reasoning

├── Evidence Evaluation

├── Constraint Analysis

├── Option Comparison

└── Final Decision
```

Composite Reasoning remains observable as a unified process.

---

# 18. Design Principles

Reasoning follows these principles.

### Goal-Oriented

Reasoning exists to achieve defined objectives.

---

### Explainable

Reasoning supports understandable conclusions.

---

### Governed

Reasoning follows platform policies.

---

### Traceable

Every conclusion preserves supporting evidence.

---

### Observable

Reasoning activities generate telemetry.

---

# 19. Reasoning Invariants

The following statements shall always remain true.

1. Every Reasoning process has a unique identity.
2. Every Reasoning process serves a defined objective.
3. Reasoning may invoke one or more Inference operations.
4. Reasoning remains governed.
5. Reasoning conclusions are traceable.
6. Reasoning activities remain observable.
7. Reasoning supports explanation.
8. Reasoning outputs never bypass platform governance.

These invariants apply throughout the cmdOS platform.

---

# 20. Relationship to the Specification

Reasoning is the canonical cognitive decision process within cmdOS.

Future specifications—including Reasoning Engine, Planning Engine, Decision Engine, AI Router, Reflection Framework, Policy Engine, Goal Management, Autonomous Agents, and Kernel—shall define behavior according to the canonical Reasoning model established in this document.

Every intelligent decision within cmdOS shall be represented as one or more Reasoning processes.

---

# 21. Summary

Reasoning is the governed cognitive process through which cmdOS transforms Knowledge, Memory, Context, and Inference into justified decisions.

It provides the decision layer of the AI Execution Operating System while remaining distinct from computation, planning, and execution.

By separating Reasoning from Inference, Planning, and Execution, cmdOS establishes an explainable, traceable, and policy-governed architecture capable of supporting trustworthy autonomous intelligence.

Reasoning is therefore the canonical cognitive decision primitive of the AI Execution Operating System.
