# Knowledge

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical
Category: Foundation

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of **Knowledge** within cmdOS.

Knowledge is the structured, governed, and reusable understanding that enables intelligent planning, reasoning, decision-making, and execution.

Knowledge transforms information into actionable understanding.

It is one of the foundational assets of the AI Execution Operating System.

---

# 2. Definition

**Knowledge** is validated information organized into a form that can be interpreted, reasoned over, shared, and applied during execution.

Knowledge may originate from:

- experience
- documentation
- observations
- external systems
- human expertise
- AI-generated analysis

Knowledge exists independently of any single execution.

---

# 3. Knowledge Is Not Data

Data represents raw facts.

Knowledge represents interpreted meaning.

```
Data

↓

Information

↓

Knowledge

↓

Execution
```

Knowledge provides understanding.

Data alone does not.

---

# 4. Knowledge Is Not Memory

Memory stores information.

Knowledge explains information.

```
Memory

↓

Stored Information

↓

Knowledge

↓

Reasoning
```

Memory preserves.

Knowledge enables intelligent action.

---

# 5. Knowledge Is Not Context

Context describes the current situation.

Knowledge represents general understanding that may apply across many situations.

```
Knowledge

↓

Used by

↓

Context

↓

Execution
```

Context changes frequently.

Knowledge is comparatively stable.

---

# 6. Knowledge Characteristics

Every Knowledge asset shall be:

- structured
- verifiable
- reusable
- governed
- observable
- versioned
- traceable

Knowledge shall never exist without provenance.

---

# 7. Knowledge Identity

Every Knowledge asset shall possess a globally unique identifier.

Minimum metadata includes:

- Knowledge ID
- Title
- Type
- Author
- Owner
- Version
- Status
- Source
- Creation Timestamp

Identity enables governance and discovery.

---

# 8. Knowledge Lifecycle

Every Knowledge asset progresses through a managed lifecycle.

```
Created

↓

Validated

↓

Published

↓

Referenced

↓

Updated

↓

Archived
```

Alternative terminal states include:

- Deprecated
- Superseded
- Removed

Lifecycle transitions shall remain observable.

---

# 9. Knowledge Sources

Knowledge may originate from multiple sources.

Examples include:

- documents
- manuals
- APIs
- databases
- execution history
- human experts
- external services
- AI analysis

Source provenance shall always be preserved.

---

# 10. Knowledge Classification

Knowledge may be classified into categories.

Examples include:

### Operational Knowledge

Execution procedures.

---

### Domain Knowledge

Industry expertise.

---

### Organizational Knowledge

Internal policies and practices.

---

### Technical Knowledge

Architecture, APIs, infrastructure.

---

### Historical Knowledge

Past executions and lessons learned.

Additional categories may be introduced without changing the architectural model.

---

# 11. Knowledge Validation

Knowledge shall be validated before publication.

Validation may include:

- source verification
- consistency checking
- policy compliance
- expert review
- automated validation

Unvalidated Knowledge shall not become authoritative.

---

# 12. Knowledge Relationships

Knowledge assets may reference:

- Documents
- Objects
- Policies
- Workflows
- Executions
- Resources
- Other Knowledge

Relationships shall remain explicit.

Hidden dependencies are prohibited.

---

# 13. Knowledge Usage

Knowledge may support:

- planning
- reasoning
- execution
- recommendation
- explanation
- optimization
- recovery

Knowledge itself does not perform execution.

It informs execution.

---

# 14. Knowledge Observability

Every significant Knowledge operation generates telemetry.

Examples include:

- created
- validated
- published
- referenced
- updated
- deprecated

Knowledge history shall remain permanently observable.

---

# 15. Knowledge Governance

Knowledge remains governed throughout its lifecycle.

Governance includes:

- ownership
- approval
- access control
- version management
- compliance
- auditing

Governance establishes trust.

---

# 16. Knowledge Versioning

Knowledge evolves through versioning.

```
Knowledge v1

↓

Knowledge v2

↓

Knowledge v3
```

Previous versions remain available.

Historical reasoning shall remain reproducible.

---

# 17. Knowledge Retrieval

Knowledge shall support efficient retrieval.

Retrieval mechanisms may include:

- semantic search
- graph traversal
- metadata filtering
- vector search
- keyword search
- hybrid retrieval

Retrieval mechanisms remain implementation independent.

---

# 18. Design Principles

Knowledge follows these principles.

### Structured

Knowledge follows defined representations.

---

### Verifiable

Knowledge is supported by identifiable sources.

---

### Reusable

Knowledge applies across multiple executions.

---

### Governed

Knowledge remains subject to platform governance.

---

### Traceable

Knowledge provenance is preserved.

---

# 19. Knowledge Invariants

The following statements shall always remain true.

1. Every Knowledge asset has a unique identity.
2. Knowledge is traceable to one or more sources.
3. Knowledge remains versioned.
4. Knowledge is governed.
5. Knowledge supports reuse.
6. Knowledge operations remain observable.
7. Validation precedes publication.
8. Knowledge informs execution but does not execute.

These invariants apply throughout the cmdOS platform.

---

# 20. Relationship to the Specification

Knowledge is the canonical representation of reusable understanding within cmdOS.

Future specifications—including Knowledge Framework, Memory Engine, Retrieval System, Reasoning Engine, Knowledge Graph, RAG Architecture, Planning Engine, and Kernel—shall define behavior according to the canonical Knowledge model established in this document.

Every reasoning process within cmdOS shall consume one or more Knowledge assets.

---

# 21. Summary

Knowledge is the structured and governed understanding that enables intelligent execution within cmdOS.

It transforms validated information into reusable insight for planning, reasoning, optimization, explanation, and decision-making.

By separating Knowledge from Data, Memory, and Context while preserving governance, provenance, and versioning, cmdOS establishes a trustworthy foundation for intelligent behavior across the AI Execution Operating System.

Knowledge is therefore the canonical reasoning primitive of the AI Execution Operating System.
