# Provider

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical
Category: Foundation

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of **Provider** within cmdOS.

A Provider is the canonical representation of an entity that supplies Resources, Capabilities, Tools, Models, Services, Infrastructure, or Data to the AI Execution Operating System.

Providers establish trust, ownership, accountability, and service boundaries between cmdOS and external or internal service ecosystems.

Every externally supplied execution dependency shall originate from a Provider.

---

# 2. Definition

A **Provider** is a governed entity responsible for delivering one or more operational services or assets to cmdOS.

A Provider may supply:

- AI Models
- APIs
- Tools
- Resources
- Connectors
- Cloud Infrastructure
- Blockchain Nodes
- Identity Services
- Storage
- Compute

Providers define service ownership rather than execution behavior.

---

# 3. Provider Is Not a Connector

Connectors establish communication.

Providers own the services being accessed.

```
Provider

↓

Connector

↓

Tool

↓

Execution
```

Connectors communicate.

Providers deliver services.

---

# 4. Provider Is Not a Resource

Resources are consumable assets.

Providers own or operate Resources.

```
Provider

↓

Resources

↓

Execution
```

Providers supply.

Resources are consumed.

---

# 5. Provider Is Not an Organization

Organizations govern cmdOS environments.

Providers supply external or internal services.

```
Organization

↓

cmdOS

↓

Provider
```

Organizations manage execution.

Providers enable execution.

---

# 6. Provider Characteristics

Every Provider shall be:

- uniquely identifiable
- governed
- discoverable
- accountable
- observable
- version independent
- trust evaluated

Providers represent service ownership.

---

# 7. Provider Identity

Every Provider shall possess a globally unique identifier.

Minimum metadata includes:

- Provider ID
- Name
- Type
- Owner
- Status
- Trust Level
- Supported Services
- Creation Timestamp

Identity enables governance and routing.

---

# 8. Provider Lifecycle

Every Provider progresses through a managed lifecycle.

```
Registered

↓

Verified

↓

Approved

↓

Available

↓

Maintained

↓

Retired
```

Alternative terminal states include:

- Suspended
- Revoked
- Removed

Lifecycle transitions shall remain observable.

# 10. Provider Services

Providers expose governed services.

Examples include:

- model inference
- authentication
- storage
- messaging
- compute
- search
- transaction execution
- monitoring

Every service shall remain associated with exactly one Provider.

---

# 11. Provider Trust

Every Provider shall possess a Trust Level.

Trust evaluation may consider:

- identity verification
- operational history
- compliance
- security posture
- reliability
- audit history

Trust influences execution decisions.

Trust does not replace authorization.

---

# 12. Provider Availability

Providers may expose operational status.

Availability states include:

- available
- degraded
- maintenance
- unavailable
- retired

Availability shall be observable.

---

# 13. Provider Routing

Multiple Providers may implement equivalent services.

Routing decisions may consider:

- latency
- cost
- trust
- availability
- policy
- geographic location

Routing algorithms remain implementation independent.

---

# 14. Provider Observability

Every significant Provider event generates telemetry.

Examples include:

- registered
- verified
- selected
- unavailable
- recovered
- retired

Provider history shall remain permanently observable.

---

# 15. Provider Governance

Providers remain governed throughout their lifecycle.

Governance includes:

- ownership
- trust evaluation
- compliance
- auditing
- policy enforcement
- contractual validation

Governance establishes platform reliability.

---

# 16. Provider Compatibility

Providers shall declare supported interfaces.

Compatibility may include:

- APIs
- protocols
- runtime versions
- authentication methods
- Connector compatibility
- Tool compatibility

Compatibility shall be validated before use.

---

# 17. Provider Version Independence

Providers may evolve independently from cmdOS.

Provider updates shall not require modification of the canonical Provider model.

Integration compatibility shall be maintained through Connectors and Tool interfaces.

---

# 18. Design Principles

Providers follow these principles.

### Accountable

Every service has an identifiable owner.

---

### Governed

Provider usage remains policy controlled.

---

### Replaceable

Equivalent Providers may substitute one another.

---

### Observable

Provider health and activity remain measurable.

---

### Trustworthy

Trust is continuously evaluated.

---

# 19. Provider Invariants

The following statements shall always remain true.

1. Every Provider has a unique identity.
2. Every service belongs to exactly one Provider.
3. Provider trust remains governed.
4. Provider availability is observable.
5. Provider compatibility is explicit.
6. Provider routing follows governance policies.
7. Provider operations remain auditable.
8. Providers never bypass platform governance.

These invariants apply throughout the cmdOS platform.

---

# 20. Relationship to the Specification

Provider is the canonical representation of service ownership within cmdOS.

Future specifications—including Provider Framework, Multi-Provider Routing, AI Router, Cost Engine, Trust Framework, Marketplace, Service Federation, Connector Framework, and Kernel—shall define behavior according to the canonical Provider model established in this document.

Every external or internal service supplier within cmdOS shall be represented as a Provider.

---

# 21. Summary

A Provider is the governed representation of an entity that supplies services, resources, infrastructure, or intelligence to cmdOS.

It establishes ownership, trust, accountability, and operational boundaries while enabling intelligent routing across multiple equivalent service providers.

By separating Providers from Connectors, Resources, and Tools, cmdOS creates a flexible architecture capable of supporting heterogeneous infrastructure, multi-model AI routing, hybrid cloud deployments, and enterprise-scale service federation.

Provider is therefore the canonical service ownership primitive of the AI Execution Operating System.
