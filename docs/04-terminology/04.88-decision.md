# Decision

Version: 1.0  
Status: Canonical  
Category: Foundation

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of **Decision** within cmdOS.

Decision is the canonical selection mechanism responsible for choosing an intended outcome from one or more possible alternatives based on Intent, Goals, Context, Knowledge, Reasoning, Policies, and Constraints.

Decision transforms intelligence into direction.

Every autonomous operation within cmdOS shall contain one or more Decision processes.

---

# 2. Definition

A **Decision** is a governed selection outcome produced after evaluating available possibilities and determining the most appropriate option according to defined objectives.

A Decision defines:

- selected option
- alternatives considered
- evaluation criteria
- confidence level
- supporting evidence
- expected outcome

Decision determines what should happen next.

---

# 3. Decision Is Not Reasoning

Reasoning evaluates information.

Decision selects an outcome.

```
Reasoning

↓

Decision

↓

Action
```

Reasoning thinks.

Decision chooses.

---

# 4. Decision Is Not Action

Action performs an operation.

Decision determines which operation should occur.

```
Decision

↓

Action

↓

Execution
```

---

# 5. Decision Is Not Intent

Intent represents desired purpose.

Decision represents the selected approach to achieve that purpose.

```
Intent

↓

Decision

↓

Execution
```

---

# 6. Decision Characteristics

Every Decision shall be:

- goal-oriented
- explainable
- traceable
- evidence-based
- confidence-aware
- governed
- reversible when possible

Decisions shall have identifiable reasoning foundations.

---

# 7. Decision Identity

Every Decision shall possess a unique identifier.

Minimum metadata includes:

- Decision ID
- Decision Maker
- Related Intent
- Context
- Reasoning Reference
- Selected Option
- Confidence
- Timestamp
- Version

Identity enables accountability.

---

Every Decision progresses through:

```

↓

↓

↓

↓

Decision Selected

↓
```

Alternative states include:

- Rejected

- Overridden

- business strategy

- investment direction

## Autonomous Decision

Created by Agents without direct human input.

Examples:

- resource allocation

## Human-Assisted Decision

Created with human involvement.

- approval

- review

## System Decision

Created by infrastructure components.

Examples:

- routing
- scheduling
- security enforcement

# 28. Relationship to the Specification

Decision is the canonical selection primitive within cmdOS.

Future specifications—including Decision Engine, Planning System, Agent Intelligence Layer, Autonomous Control Framework, and Cognitive Architecture—shall define behavior according to the canonical Decision model established in this document.

Every autonomous capability within cmdOS depends on Decision mechanisms.

---

# 29. Summary

Decision provides the choice layer of cmdOS.

It connects Reasoning, Knowledge, Intent, Planning, Actions, Execution, and Learning while enabling Agents to determine what should happen next.

By separating Decision from Reasoning, Action, and Execution, cmdOS establishes a foundation for autonomous systems capable of thinking, choosing, and acting responsibly.

Decision is therefore the canonical selection primitive of the AI Execution Operating System.
