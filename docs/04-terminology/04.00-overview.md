# Terminology Overview

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical
Category: Foundation

---

# 1. Purpose

This chapter establishes the canonical vocabulary of cmdOS.

Every specification, implementation, API, SDK, plugin, runtime component, and architectural document shall use the terminology defined here.

A shared language is essential for building a coherent execution platform.

---

# 2. Why Terminology Matters

Large systems fail when different teams use the same words to mean different things.

Examples include:

- "Agent"
- "Task"
- "Execution"
- "Capability"
- "Workflow"

Without precise definitions:

- documentation becomes inconsistent
- APIs become ambiguous
- implementations diverge
- architecture becomes fragmented

Canonical terminology eliminates ambiguity.

---

# 3. Scope

This chapter defines the official meaning of the fundamental concepts used throughout cmdOS.

These definitions apply to:

- specifications
- documentation
- source code
- APIs
- SDKs
- plugins
- user interfaces
- developer tools
- operational procedures

Alternative definitions shall not be introduced without architectural approval.

---

# 4. Terminology Hierarchy

The cmdOS vocabulary follows a layered model.

```
Human Intent

Intent

Planning

↓

Workflow

↓

Capability
```

Higher-level concepts describe objectives.

Lower-level concepts describe execution.

---

# 5. Normative Language

This specification adopts the following terminology conventions.

### SHALL

A mandatory requirement.

---

### SHALL NOT

A prohibited behavior.

---

### SHOULD

A strong recommendation.

Exceptions require justification.

---

### SHOULD NOT

Generally discouraged.

Exceptions require documented reasoning.

---

### MAY

An optional capability.

Implementations remain compliant whether or not the feature exists.

# 7. Naming Principles

Terminology should satisfy the following principles.

### Precise

Every term has one meaning.

---

### Consistent

One concept uses one name.

---

### Stable

Definitions evolve slowly.

---

### Technology Neutral

Definitions describe architectural concepts rather than implementation details.

---

### Human Readable

Terminology should be understandable by engineers, architects, operators, and AI systems.

---

# 8. Relationship to Other Chapters

The definitions introduced here become normative references for every remaining specification.

Future chapters shall reference these definitions rather than redefining them.

If clarification is required, the canonical terminology shall be updated instead of creating competing definitions.

---

# 9. Constitutional Authority

The terminology defined in this chapter forms part of the constitutional specification of cmdOS.

Conflicting terminology shall be considered non-compliant unless explicitly approved through architectural governance.

---

# 10. Summary

Architecture depends upon shared language.

The purpose of this chapter is to establish a precise, stable, and technology-neutral vocabulary for the AI Execution Operating System.

Every specification that follows assumes the terminology defined here.
