# Actor

Version: 1.0  
Status: Canonical  
Category: Foundation

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of **Actor** within cmdOS.

An Actor is the canonical representation of an entity capable of initiating Intent, making Decisions, requesting Executions, or interacting with the AI Execution Operating System.

Actors represent sources of agency within cmdOS.

Every intentional activity within cmdOS shall originate from one or more Actors.

---

# 2. Definition

An **Actor** is an Identity-bearing entity that possesses the ability to initiate, influence, authorize, or participate in platform operations.

An Actor may:

- create Intent
- define Goals
- approve Decisions
- initiate Executions
- interact with Objects
- delegate authority
- consume Results

Actors represent agency.

They do not necessarily perform execution directly.

---

# 3. Actor Is Not an Object

Objects represent entities that exist.

Actors represent entities capable of intentional interaction.

```
Object

↓

Identity

↓

Actor
```

An Object may have Identity.

An Identity becomes an Actor when it possesses agency.

---

# 4. Actor Is Not Identity

Identity defines who or what exists.

Actor defines who or what can cause change.

```
Identity

↓

Actor

↓

Action
```

Not every Identity is an Actor.

---

# 5. Actor Is Not an Agent

Agents are one type of Actor.

Actors include humans, AI systems, and automated services.

```
Actor

├── Human Actor

├── Agent Actor

├── Service Actor

└── External Actor
```

Agents are autonomous Actors.

---

# 6. Actor Characteristics

Every Actor shall be:

- identifiable
- authorized
- accountable
- observable
- governable
- traceable
- permission-controlled

Actors operate within defined authority boundaries.

---

# 7. Actor Identity

Every Actor shall possess an Identity.

Minimum metadata includes:

- Actor ID
- Identity ID
- Actor Type
- Owner
- Authority Scope
- Status
- Creation Timestamp

Identity provides accountability.

---

# 8. Actor Types

cmdOS recognizes multiple Actor categories.

---

## Human Actor

Represents human participants.

Examples:

- users
- administrators
- developers
- operators

Human Actors provide direct intent and approval.

---

## Agent Actor

Represents autonomous AI entities.

Examples:

- personal assistants
- trading agents
- workflow agents
- research agents

Agent Actors may reason and execute within permissions.

---

## Service Actor

Represents automated software systems.

Examples:

- background services
- automation workers
- system processes

---

## External Actor

Represents external entities interacting with cmdOS.

Examples:

- third-party systems
- partner platforms
- external applications

---

# 9. Actor Lifecycle

Every Actor progresses through a managed lifecycle.

```
Created

↓

Verified

↓

Active

↓

Restricted

↓

Suspended

↓

Revoked
```

Alternative terminal states include:

- Expired
- Removed

Lifecycle transitions shall remain observable.

---

# 10. Actor Authority

Every Actor operates under defined authority.

Authority determines:

- available Actions
- accessible Objects
- allowed Capabilities
- execution limits
- delegation rights

Authority shall always be explicit.

---

# 11. Actor Intent

Actors may create Intent.

```
Actor

↓

Intent

↓

Goal

↓

Execution
```

Intent represents desired outcomes initiated by Actors.

---

# 12. Actor Decision

Actors may produce or approve Decisions.

Decision authority depends on:

- permissions
- trust level
- organizational policy
- approval requirements

Not every Actor may create final Decisions.

---

# 13. Actor Delegation

Actors may delegate authority.

Example:

```
Human Actor

↓

Agent Actor

↓

Tool Execution
```

Delegation shall define:

- scope
- duration
- limitations
- accountability

Delegated authority never exceeds the original authority.

---

# 14. Actor Permissions

Actor permissions define allowed operations.

Permissions may include:

- create
- read
- update
- execute
- approve
- delegate
- administer

Permissions are enforced by the Authorization Layer.

---

# 15. Actor Trust

Actors may possess Trust Levels.

Trust evaluation may consider:

- identity verification
- historical behavior
- reputation
- compliance
- security posture

Trust influences available authority.

---

# 16. Actor Observability

Every significant Actor operation generates telemetry.

Examples include:

- created
- authenticated
- requested execution
- delegated authority
- approved decision
- revoked

Actor history shall remain permanently observable.

---

# 17. Actor Governance

Actors remain governed throughout their lifecycle.

Governance includes:

- identity verification
- permission management
- authority control
- auditing
- compliance
- accountability

Governance establishes responsible agency.

---

# 18. Actor Interaction Model

Actors interact with cmdOS through:

```
Actor

↓

Intent

↓

Reasoning

↓

Decision

↓

Planning

↓

Execution
```

Actors provide the origin of intentional behavior.

---

# 19. Design Principles

Actors follow these principles.

### Accountable

Every action has an attributable source.

---

### Governed

Actor authority is controlled.

---

### Traceable

Actor behavior remains observable.

---

### Delegatable

Authority may be safely delegated.

---

### Secure

Actors operate within explicit boundaries.

---

# 20. Actor Invariants

The following statements shall always remain true.

1. Every Actor has an Identity.
2. Every Actor has explicit authority.
3. Every Actor operation is traceable.
4. Actor permissions are governed.
5. Actor delegation is controlled.
6. Actor lifecycle is observable.
7. Actors cannot exceed granted authority.
8. Actors cannot bypass security governance.

These invariants apply throughout the cmdOS platform.

---

# 21. Relationship to the Specification

Actor is the canonical agency primitive within cmdOS.

Future specifications—including Actor Framework, Identity System, Authorization Engine, Agent Architecture, Delegation Framework, Human-in-the-Loop System, Security Layer, and Kernel—shall define behavior according to the canonical Actor model established in this document.

Every source of intentional behavior within cmdOS shall be represented as an Actor.

---

# 22. Summary

An Actor is the entity capable of initiating change within cmdOS.

It connects Identity with Intent, Decision, and Execution while maintaining authority, accountability, security, and governance.

By separating Actors from Objects, Identities, and Agents, cmdOS establishes a universal model for human participation, autonomous AI behavior, and automated system interaction.

Actor is therefore the canonical agency primitive of the AI Execution Operating System.
