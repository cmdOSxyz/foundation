# Organization

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical
Category: Foundation

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the canonical meaning of **Organization** within cmdOS.

An Organization is the highest administrative and governance boundary within the AI Execution Operating System.

Organizations own identities, resources, policies, projects, workspaces, and administrative authority.

They provide isolation, governance, accountability, and operational ownership for groups of identities operating within cmdOS.

---

# 2. Definition

An **Organization** is a governed administrative entity that owns and manages resources, identities, permissions, policies, and operational environments.

Organizations establish ownership boundaries.

Every governed asset belongs to exactly one Organization.

---

# 3. Organization Is Not an Identity

Organizations possess Identity.

Organizations are not individual actors.

```
Organization

↓

Organization Identity

↓

Governed Operations
```

The Organization acts through authorized Identities.

---

# 4. Organization Is Not a Project

Projects are operational units.

Organizations own Projects.

```
Organization

↓

Projects

↓

Workspaces

↓

Executions
```

Projects exist within Organizations.

---

# 5. Organization Is Not a Workspace

A Workspace provides an operational environment.

Organizations may contain many Workspaces.

```
Organization

├── Workspace A

├── Workspace B

└── Workspace C
```

Workspaces inherit governance from their Organization.

---

# 6. Organization Characteristics

Every Organization shall be:

- uniquely identifiable
- governable
- observable
- isolated
- scalable
- versioned
- accountable

Organizations are persistent platform entities.

---

# 7. Organization Identity

Every Organization shall possess a globally unique identifier.

Minimum metadata includes:

- Organization ID
- Name
- Display Name
- Status
- Owner
- Creation Timestamp
- Version
- Trust Level

Organization identity remains permanent.

---

# 8. Organization Lifecycle

Every Organization progresses through a managed lifecycle.

```
Registered

↓

Verified

↓

Active

↓

Suspended

↓

Reactivated

↓

Archived
```

Alternative terminal states include:

- Dissolved
- Merged
- Deleted

Lifecycle transitions shall remain observable.

---

# 9. Organization Ownership

Organizations own governed platform assets.

Examples include:

- Identities
- Projects
- Workspaces
- Resources
- Policies
- Roles
- Permissions
- Executions
- Billing Accounts

Ownership determines governance authority.

---

# 10. Organization Membership

Identities may belong to one or more Organizations.

Membership shall define:

- member identity
- membership type
- assigned roles
- delegated authority
- effective duration

Membership remains governed.

---

# 11. Organization Hierarchy

Organizations may establish hierarchical structures.

Example:

```
Enterprise

↓

Business Unit

↓

Department

↓

Team
```

Hierarchies shall remain explicit.

Circular organizational relationships are prohibited.

---

# 12. Organization Governance

Organizations define governance for owned assets.

Governance includes:

- policies
- permissions
- resource ownership
- administrative authority
- compliance requirements
- security standards

Organization governance operates within platform governance.

---

# 13. Organization Isolation

Organizations remain logically isolated.

Isolation protects:

- resources
- identities
- billing
- policies
- execution history
- operational data

Cross-organization access requires explicit authorization.

---

# 14. Organization Observability

Every Organization generates governance telemetry.

Examples include:

- organization created
- member added
- policy updated
- resource allocated
- workspace created
- organization suspended

Administrative history shall remain permanently observable.

---

# 15. Organization Administration

Organizations may designate administrative identities.

Administrative authority may include:

- user management
- policy management
- resource allocation
- billing management
- security administration
- compliance reporting

Administrative authority shall remain governed.

---

# 16. Organization Trust

Organizations possess Trust characteristics.

Trust may consider:

- verification status
- security posture
- compliance history
- operational behavior
- governance maturity
- platform reputation

Trust influences governance decisions.

Trust does not replace Policy evaluation.

---

# 17. Organization Federation

Organizations may establish governed relationships.

Examples include:

- supplier partnerships
- customer organizations
- delegated administration
- federated identity
- shared projects

Federation requires explicit governance agreements.

---

# 18. Design Principles

Organizations follow these principles.

### Governed

Organizations operate under platform governance.

---

### Isolated

Organizations remain logically separated.

---

### Accountable

Every governed asset has an owning Organization.

---

### Observable

Administrative activities generate telemetry.

---

### Scalable

Organizations support growth without architectural change.

---

# 19. Organization Invariants

The following statements shall always remain true.

1. Every Organization has a unique identity.
2. Every governed asset belongs to an Organization.
3. Organizations define ownership boundaries.
4. Organization governance remains observable.
5. Organizational isolation is enforced.
6. Membership is explicit.
7. Administrative authority remains governed.
8. Platform governance has ultimate authority.

These invariants apply throughout the cmdOS platform.

---

# 20. Relationship to the Specification

Organization is the canonical administrative boundary within cmdOS.

Future specifications—including Organization Framework, Identity Framework, RBAC, Resource Manager, Billing System, Workspace Architecture, Multi-Tenant Architecture, Trust Framework, Security Architecture, and Kernel—shall define behavior according to the canonical Organization model established in this document.

Every enterprise deployment of cmdOS shall organize governance through Organizations.

---

# 21. Summary

An Organization is the highest administrative and governance entity within cmdOS.

It establishes ownership, governance, isolation, and accountability for identities, resources, policies, projects, and operational environments.

By separating organizational governance from platform governance while maintaining explicit ownership and trust relationships, cmdOS enables secure, scalable, and enterprise-ready multi-tenant operation.

Organization is therefore the canonical governance boundary of the AI Execution Operating System.
